Parasocial Relationships

And why we should talk about them more.



What are Parasocial Relationships?

Parasocial relationships are relationships that are one sided in nature, meaning that the relationship is not mutual. One person in the relationship almost idolizes the other person, spending time, emotional energy, and obsessing over the other person. On the other hand, the other person in the "relationship" is compelety unaware of the other person's existence.



Why are Parasocial Relationships Bad?

On paper, Parasocial relationships aren't that bad. I mean, who doesn't have a favorite celebrity? Celebrities are naturally charsimatic. It starts to get dangerous when people start to believe that they truly know the celebrity, and the celebrity truly knows and cares about them. When this affection is not returned, this can lead the fan to feel unrightfully entitled to that person,which can deepen feelings of loneliness that most likely lead to the relationship in the first face. This can also lead the fan to make rash decisions, such as harassment of the celebrity, stalking them, or even worse.




Different Types of Parasocial Relationships

Research in 2006 by David Giles and John Maltby classifed parasocial relationships into three different catagories.


1. Entertainment-Social:

The most common type of parasocial relationship. You know the celebrity and find them entertaining, and interact with their work because of it, but that's where it ends.


2. Intense-personal:

In a intense-personal relationships, the person is aware they don't really know the celebrity, but that doesn't stop from worshipping that celebrity. A person in this relationship feels the need to check that celebrity's social media everyday, which can cause them to not form close bonds with the people in real life.


3. Borderline-pathological:

The least common and most dangerous form of parasocial relationship. A person in this relationship is fully delusional in the belief that the celebrity knows about them, and that they would also die for their favorite celebrity. This relationship usually leads to stalking.

How to stop having Parasocial Relationships

If your ever in a postion where you realise that you might be in a Parasocial Relationship, here are a few steps you can take to better yourself.


1. Get off social media

Like it or not, most social media revolves around celebrity culture. Unplugging and spending time off social media can help you clear your head.


2.Make some friends

Having friends outside the internet can help you make true bonds with others, as well as find people with the same passions as you.


3.Find a hobby

No person's life to revole around just one person. Finding things you're passionate about can give a sense of identity, as well as giving you a positive outlet for your emotions.



4.Touch some grass(go outside)

Science has proven that if humans do not go outside for a long experience,they will go insane(trust me). As much as you internet dwellers refuse to admit it, humans thrive in nature. Even if its just for a second, spend some time outside, and breathe some fresh air.



5. Go to Therapy

Intense parasocial relationships usually start from a dissatisfaction in one's actual life. Going to therapy can help figure out those underlying issues.
